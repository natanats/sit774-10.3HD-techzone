CHIP COMPARISON

Gaming PCs typically use high-performance desktop CPUs such as Intel Core i9 or AMD Ryzen 9, designed for maximum power, high clock speeds, and strong multi-core performance. These chips excel in gaming, heavy multitasking, and demanding workloads but require more power and cooling. In contrast, MacBook devices use Apple’s M-series chips (like M2 or M3), which are based on ARM architecture. These chips focus on efficiency, delivering strong performance while consuming less power. They integrate CPU, GPU, and memory into a single system, making them ideal for productivity, creative work, and long battery life.
